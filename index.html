Profile

Lavanya Bhatnagar is an undergraduate researcher in Mechanical Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology Indore, working at the intersection of autonomous navigation, perception and embedded hardware. Her work spans aerial and ground robotic platforms, with an emphasis on carrying algorithms validated in simulation through to field-deployable systems.

Her technical practice extends across the autonomy stack — from sensor fusion and real-time perception pipelines to flight-controller integration and embedded firmware — on platforms including the Pixhawk, NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano and ESP32.

She favours rigorous validation over assumption: quantifying system performance through controlled experimentation, and documenting the trade-offs between accuracy, latency and onboard power constraints that shape any real deployment.
